Self-Employed / Freelancer PIT

Unlike salaried workers, self-employed Nigerians calculate and file their own Personal Income Tax directly with their State Internal Revenue Service.

NTA 2025: Your taxable income = Gross Income − Business Expenses − Pension − Rent Relief (20% of rent, max ₦500,000). First ₦800,000 of taxable income is tax-free. File with your State IRS by March 31 each year.

Recent Tax Updates & Trends

Key developments in Nigerian tax law and FIRS policy you should know about.

Nigeria Tax Act 2025 — Effective Jan 1, 2026
PAYE Overhaul: CRA Abolished, New Tax Bands
The Consolidated Relief Allowance is gone. It is replaced by a Rent Relief (20% of annual rent, capped at ₦500,000). New progressive bands: 0% on first ₦800,000, then 15%–25% on higher income. Top rate reduced from 24% to 25% but structured more progressively.
Nigeria Tax Act 2025 — Effective Jan 1, 2026
Small Company CIT Threshold Raised to ₦100M
Small companies are now defined as those with annual turnover not exceeding ₦100M AND fixed assets not exceeding ₦250M. They are fully exempt from CIT, CGT, and the new Development Levy. Note: professional services firms do NOT qualify for this exemption.
Nigeria Tax Act 2025 — Effective Jan 1, 2026
New 4% Development Levy Replaces Multiple Taxes
The Tertiary Education Tax (2.5%), IT Levy, NASENI Levy, and Police Trust Fund Levy are all consolidated into a single 4% Development Levy on assessable profits. Simplifies compliance but effectively increases the levy burden for some companies.
Nigeria Tax Act 2025 — Effective Jan 1, 2026
FIRS Renamed: Now Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS)
The Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) has been restructured and renamed the Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS). TIN is now mandatory for all financial transactions, with an individual's NIN serving as their Tax ID. A new Tax Ombuds office handles taxpayer complaints independently.
Nigeria Tax Act 2025 — Effective Jan 1, 2026
Digital Economy Taxation Expanded
Non-resident companies providing digital services to Nigerian consumers must register with the NRS, charge VAT, and remit accordingly. This affects streaming platforms, app stores, SaaS providers, and cloud services. E-invoicing (fiscalisation) is now mandatory for VAT-registered businesses.
Nigeria Tax Act 2025 — Effective Jan 1, 2026
CIT Rate Reduced to 25% for Non-Small Companies
The previous two-tier structure (20% medium, 30% large) is replaced by a single 25% rate for all non-small companies. This applies from the 2026 year of assessment. CGT for companies is now also taxed at the CIT rate (30% → 25%), while CGT for individuals follows the same progressive PIT bands.
